Welcome to the Breast Cancer Detection System

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that originates in the cells of the breast. It occurs when breast cells undergo abnormal growth and division, leading to the formation of a tumor. If left untreated, breast cancer can spread to other parts of the body, a process known as metastasis. It is one of the most common cancers affecting women, but it can also occur in men, although rarely.

Causes And Risk Factors

The exact cause of breast cancer is not fully understood, but several risk factors have been identified, including:

  • Genetic Factors: Mutations in genes like BRCA1 and BRCA2 increase the risk of developing breast cancer.
  • Hormonal Influence: High levels of estrogen and progesterone can contribute to abnormal cell growth in the breast.
  • Lifestyle Factors: Poor diet, lack of physical activity, alcohol consumption, and smoking increase the risk.
  • Family History: Having a close relative with breast cancer raises the likelihood of developing the disease.
  • Age and Gender: The risk increases with age, and women are more prone to breast cancer than men.

Symptoms of Breast Cancer

Common signs and symptoms of breast cancer include:

  • A lump or thickening in the breast or underarm
  • Changes in breast size, shape, or appearance
  • Unexplained pain in the breast or nipple
  • Skin changes (redness, dimpling, or puckering)
  • Nipple discharge, including blood
  • Inverted or retracted nipple
